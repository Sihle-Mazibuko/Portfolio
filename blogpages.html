Context and Convention

And this brings me to the UI/UX text where the main message was users should be able to fully enjoy your website. It is not only about having a page that shows something, that page should make people want to visit it again if not stay longer than they currently have. This can be achieved by paying attention to important UI/UX elements such as responsiveness, the sizes of different types of information, and particular events that can be performed by the user (through mouse and keyboard inputs) that may affect their experience with the website. The final thought to draw upon is why conventions are important. Steven Mailloux mentioned how traditional conventions capture an essential aspect of our everyday notion of social convention so it is important to never disregard something simple websites practises (such as the back to top button traditionally at the bottom right of every screen). As much as it is your website and you always want to add your touch to things it is important to not stray too far away from the norm because some things are the way they are due to them being a socially acceptable functionality.

Mailloux, S. (1983). Convention and Context. New Literary History, 14(2), 399–407. https://doi.org/10.2307/468693

The Ethics of UX Practice:

The slides and lecture on user experience ethics provided useful insight into the techniques used by major corporations when designing their websites. In web design, most will agree the main aim is to create space that not only gives efficient information to your user but also does this using functionality techniques that are easily accessible and comprehendible by all who access the website. With companies, however, this seems to be a lost practice as the main aim of a business is to maximize profit. Unfortunately for the user, maximising profit and being ethical in design practices are not always synonymous. This is why designers who are aware of design ethics need to step into place ethics that benefit user experience above design techniques that seek only the user’s money to gain profits.

Digital Hegemonies:

This reading makes one aware of the inequalities that exist regarding internet access around the world and the repercussions. These areas are predominantly countries to be in “The South”, “third world countries” and “developing countries” mostly found in South America, Africa, and Asia. The main ones are the lack of knowledge being prevalent in such areas as well as the lack of information that can be distributed to these areas. Another interesting point is how certain areas are fed certain information depending on the location. This is of course debatable if it is good or bad as being shown content relevant to where you are can help reduce an overload of information that was not requested and is irrelevant but also it raises concerns regarding freedom of access to the internet.

information Geographies:

Week 12:

APIs:

APIs are new and interesting concept as they are essentially a way of getting information from a source and implementing it into the current site. This enables information to be effectively communicated as content and will be accurately depicted on the site, with fewer hassles about efficiency as whenever the APIs information is adjusted in any manner there is minimal change that needs to occur on the site. However, relying on an external source has its issues as you may not be always in control of changes that may occur leading to complications with data on your site, and security concerns may also arise when accessing APIs.

For my site, I could see APIs being useful in the portfolio section, instead of requiring me to manually input all the works and information (even though this is safer to do) I can access it from a source and display it. Whenever I change a project in any way I only have to deal with the source and not the site. Another area could be the blogs as they can be stored and gathered from a specific area. APIs are a complex concept, especially in implementation however it will be interesting to see the different ways they can be implemented.

Travelling While Trans:

Constanza-Chock gave a heartfelt breakdown of an incident she fell victim to which was caused by her identification of being part of the LGBTQI+ community. Identifying as a transgender woman, she could not be properly “classified” at airport security as a machine can only see cis males and females. This incident provides a good structure to how technology and how we understand it requires updating as times have changed, information has increased and knowledge regarding different ideologies has increased. This is important according to Constanza-Chock as “design justice” is required as we need to be inclusive in the space we work in as more individuals with different backgrounds and knowledge are going to interact with what is made and thus understanding is required. Allowing varied minds can lead to better innovation as more ideas breed better creativity and more expansive thoughts.

Costanza-Chock, S. (2020) Design Justice: Community-Led Practices to Build the Worlds We Need. Cambridge, MA, USA: MIT Press

Internet, Society and Design Justice:

Design Justice:

A key point I have noted is that the reason we design is to empower and fix the communities we design to break the shackles of exploitative systems. This is by prioritizing the opinions of those who are impacted by design inequalities. This is important as prioritizing those designs has an impact on communities thus change is necessary. This change also comes from being accountable for previous mistakes, making systems more accessible and partnering with new and excluded minds to challenge conventional methods of design. Designers have this power due to them being facilitators in the design process and not only experts. Every designer is an expert due to having their own life experiences however it is crucial to use these experiences to share design knowledge and tools to become facilitators who strive for sustainability, community-led and controlled design outcomes.

Decolonising Design:

This is accomplished by using imperative strategies, ending the currently used cycle of knowledge production and ensuring “reproduction of validity”. Areas should be created for designers and design researchers outside the standard Anglo-European field. Power relations and logic assertions found in technologies and techniques should be critiqued. The introduction of different mindsets when having discourses should be encouraged in design discourses and we should be thinking outside of the designs we have today. Design systems made should aim to be alternatives to neocolonial world systems. Individuals and minds that have suffered and are thus the colonised need to have a say as to what constitutes design and also what design can be.
